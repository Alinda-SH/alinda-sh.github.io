Definition

motivation
[ moh-tuh-vey-shuhn ]
noun
the act or an instance of motivating, or providing with a reason to act in a certain way:
I don't understand what her motivation was for quitting her job.
the state or condition of being motivated or having a strong reason to act or accomplish something:
We know that these students have strong motivation to learn.
something that motivates; inducement; incentive:
Clearly, the company's long-term motivation is profit.

What is motivation?

According to author and speaker Kendra Cherry (Cherry, What is motivation?, 2020) is motivation a process that initiates, guides and maintains goal-oriented behaviours. It is what causes you to act, whether it is getting a glass of water to reduce thirst or reading a book to gain knowledge.

Motivation involves the emotional, biological, cognitive, and social forces that initiate and activate behaviour. In everyday practise, the term “motivation” is often used to describe why a person does something.

Cherry claims motivation doesn’t only refer to the factors that activate behaviour, it also involves the aspects that direct and maintain these goal-directed actions, though such causes are rarely directly observable). As an outcome, we frequently have to infer the reasons why people do things they do based on observable behaviours.
